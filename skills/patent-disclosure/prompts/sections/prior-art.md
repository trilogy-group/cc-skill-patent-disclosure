# Prior Art — Section Prompt

## Question
What existing patents, papers, products, and techniques are known to relate to this invention?

## Prompt

Document all prior art that the inventors are aware of. This section captures what the inventor KNOWS about — it is NOT a prior art search. Completeness matters: failure to disclose known prior art can invalidate a patent.

**Novelty Statement:**
{novelty_statement}

**User-Provided Prior Art References:**
{prior_art_from_qa}

**Requirements:**

1. **For each known prior art reference, provide:**
   - **Type**: Patent / Paper / Product / Open-source project / Industry technique
   - **Reference**: Name, author/company, date, URL or patent number if known
   - **Description**: What it does (2-3 sentences)
   - **Relationship to this invention**: How is it related? Does it address the same problem? Use a similar technique?
   - **Key differences**: What specifically does THIS invention do differently?

2. **Categories to cover:**
   - **Company's own prior products/features** that address the same problem space
   - **Competitor products** with similar functionality
   - **Academic papers or algorithms** that the invention builds upon
   - **Open-source projects** that do something similar
   - **Industry-standard techniques** that are related

3. **If the user provided no prior art references:**
   - State: "The inventors have not identified specific prior art references."
   - Still list the general known approaches from the Alternatives section, formatted as prior art entries.
   - Note: "A formal prior art search has not been conducted and is recommended before filing."

**IMPORTANT:**
- Only include references the inventors are actually aware of (from Q&A Round 3)
- Do NOT conduct a web search for prior art — this creates duty-of-disclosure obligations
- Do NOT fabricate or hallucinate patent numbers or paper citations
- If uncertain whether something exists, say "the inventors believe that..." rather than stating it as fact

**Length:** Variable — depends on how much prior art the inventor is aware of. Can be as short as a single paragraph if no specific references are known.
