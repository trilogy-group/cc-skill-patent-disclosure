# Problems Solved — Section Prompt

## Question
What specific problems does this invention solve, and why were prior approaches inadequate?

## Prompt

Articulate the problems this invention addresses. Frame them from the perspective of someone who had to live with the old way of doing things — make the pain real and specific.

**Novelty Statement:**
{novelty_statement}

**Requirements:**

1. **Primary Problem** — State the main problem in concrete terms:
   - What specifically was failing, slow, inaccurate, costly, or impossible?
   - Quantify where possible (e.g., "false positive rate exceeded 30%", "latency exceeded 500ms at 10K QPS")
   - Who experienced this problem? (engineers, end users, operations teams?)

2. **Root Cause** — Why did this problem exist? What fundamental limitation in prior approaches caused it?

3. **Secondary Problems** — List any additional problems solved as a consequence of the primary solution

4. **Prior Approaches and Their Shortcomings** — For each known prior approach:
   - What was the approach?
   - How did it attempt to solve the problem?
   - Specifically why was it inadequate? (not just "it didn't work" — explain the mechanism of failure)
   - What tradeoffs did it force?

5. **Impact of the Problem** — What was the business/technical consequence of not having this invention?
   - Cost (compute, engineering time, opportunity cost)
   - Quality degradation
   - Scalability ceiling
   - User experience impact

**Anti-patterns:**
- Do NOT describe problems in abstract terms — be concrete and specific
- Do NOT invent problems that didn't actually exist — only describe real pain points the engineer experienced
- Do NOT exaggerate — measured understatement is more credible
