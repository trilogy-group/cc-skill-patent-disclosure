# Novelty — Section Prompt

## Question
What is genuinely new about this invention? What is the inventive step?

## Prompt

Articulate the novelty of the invention with precision and specificity. This section is the most critical part of the disclosure — it defines what the patent will actually protect.

**Requirements:**

1. **The Inventive Concept** — State in 2-3 sentences exactly what is new. Not what the system does, but what about HOW it does it is different from anything that existed before.

2. **Differentiation from Known Approaches** — For each known approach to the same problem, state specifically what this invention does differently. Use concrete technical comparisons:
   - "Unlike X which does A, this invention does B, which achieves C"
   - Be specific about the mechanism, not just the result

3. **Non-Obvious Elements** — Identify aspects that would not be obvious to a skilled engineer. What would surprise someone in this field? What counter-intuitive choices were made and why do they work?

4. **Scope of Novelty** — Define the boundaries:
   - What is the broadest defensible claim? (the general principle)
   - What is the narrowest specific implementation? (the exact technique)
   - Are there intermediate scope levels worth protecting?

5. **Technical Advantage** — What measurable or observable improvement does this novelty provide? (Speed, accuracy, cost, scalability, reliability, etc.)

**Anti-patterns to avoid:**
- Do NOT conflate "new to us" with "novel" — it must be new to the field
- Do NOT describe standard engineering practices as novel (using a database, caching, REST APIs, etc.)
- Do NOT overstate — understated, precise claims are stronger than broad, vague ones
- Do NOT mix novelty with utility — this section is about WHAT is new, not WHY it's useful
